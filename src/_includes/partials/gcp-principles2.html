The 13 Good Clinical Practice Principles

1. Ethics:

Clinical trials must follow ethical principles outlined in the Declaration of Helsinki, Good Clinical Practice (GCP), and relevant regulations.

2. Trial risk vs. trial benefit:

Before starting a trial, weigh potential risks and inconveniences against the anticipated benefits for individual participants and society as a whole. Initiate and continue the trial only if the benefits outweigh the risks.

3. Trial participants:

The rights, safety, and well-being of trial subjects are the top priority, taking precedence over scientific or societal interests.

4. Information on medicinal products:

Sufficient non-clinical and clinical data on the investigational product must be available to justify the proposed clinical trial.

5. Good quality trials:

Clinical trials should be scientifically sound and meticulously described in a clear and detailed protocol.

6. Compliance with the study protocol:

The trial must be conducted strictly according to the protocol, which has been previously approved by an Institutional Review Board (IRB) or Independent Ethics Committee (IEC).

7. Medicinal decisions:

Qualified physicians (or dentists, when appropriate) are always responsible for medical care and decisions made on behalf of trial subjects.

8. Trial staff:

Everyone involved in conducting the trial must possess the necessary education, training, and experience to perform their designated tasks competently.

9. Informed consent:

Freely given informed consent must be obtained from every subject before participating in a clinical trial.

10. Clinical trial data:

All clinical trial information should be recorded, managed, and stored in a way that enables accurate reporting, interpretation, and verification.

11. Confidentiality:

Maintain the confidentiality of records that could identify subjects, respecting privacy and confidentiality rules as per applicable regulations.

12. Good manufacturing practice:

Investigational products must be manufactured, handled, and stored following Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) guidelines. They must be used strictly according to the approved protocol.

13. Quality assurance:

Implement systems with procedures to ensure the quality of every aspect of the trial.
